About Me

I first learned to code during night shifts in the hospital laboratory where I worked as a Medical Technologist. A year later I entered an accelerated Computer Science postbac program at Portland State University, where I later graduated with a Masters of Computer Science. During my Masters I developed a love for functional programming and completed two specialization tracks (Databases and Languages and Programming), two internships, and a TA position (for the Full Stack Web Development and Front End Web Technologies courses).

In practice, I'm a full stack developer who enjoys trying out new techniques and improving the effeciency, stability, and safety of existing code. I have a strong understanding of the theoretical and practical side of several programming paradigms, which allows me to identify alternative solutions to problems depending on how much factors like effeciency and stability should be prioritized.

When I'm not coding I enjoy vegan cooking, adding books to my ever-growing 'to-read' pile, and playing with my two cats. I have an abiding love of fantasy games and let out my creativity by programming my own old-school rougelikes and running a Dungeons and Dragons game.
